How it works

From map to meetup in four simple steps.

Echo keeps the flow clear so users understand what happens before they share details or meet anyone. The product is built for people who want practice that feels natural, safe, and easy to start.

01

Open the Map

Browse nearby language meetups, hosts, and practice-friendly locations.

02

Join or Host Events

Choose casual events with visible time, place, language, and skill level.

03

Chat & Translate

Message before meeting and use translation support when the conversation gets stuck.

04

Grow Naturally

Build confidence by speaking with people instead of only studying alone.

What users see

Every decision is visible before someone commits.

Echo is intentionally transparent about distance, format, group size, and language level so people can make a comfortable choice.

Location context

Meetups appear around real places such as cafes, libraries, parks, and clubs so users know what kind of environment to expect.

Host signals

Profiles can show language level, spoken languages, and host status to help learners decide who feels right to meet.

Simple timing

Events show time, expected length, and whether the session is drop-in friendly or better for a full conversation.

Lower pressure

Users can chat first, bring a friend, join group sessions, and choose public venues before moving into deeper practice.

Before, during, after

Echo supports the whole meetup moment.

Before meeting, users can introduce themselves and translate simple messages. During the meetup, the event page keeps the language goal clear. Afterward, people can reconnect, join another session, or host their own.

BeforeMessage the host, confirm the language, and check the venue.
DuringUse simple prompts, translation support, and visible skill levels.
AfterFollow people you met and find the next local conversation.
